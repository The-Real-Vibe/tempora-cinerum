Tempora Cinerum

Viaticum

n. — provision for a journey; the rite given to one about to depart.

He was going to find Aki when he got back. He was going to tell her about the light through the canvas in the hour before dawn, the color of it. Not quite gold. Something thinner than gold. He’d been keeping that image for eight weeks.

He had twelve days left. A season of small things saved up to carry home, and twelve days to do it. He noticed the morning was wrong, and filed it away to deal with later, the way he always had. There wasn’t going to be a later.
